Sampling Frequency Effects in DC Motor Control

Experimental Study of Discrete-Time PI Control on DC Motor Response

Executive Summary

Experimentally investigated how discrete-time controller sampling frequency influences closed-loop DC motor speed regulation, focusing on tracking accuracy and transient behavior with a PI controller across a range of sample rates.

Problem & Purpose

In digital control systems, sampling frequency fundamentally affects controller performance. This study quantifies how sampling rates from low (~10 Hz) to high (~100 Hz) impact RMS tracking error, transient response, overshoot, and settling time in a DC motor speed regulation task.

Experimental Setup

  • Controller: Discrete-time PI controller implemented on a microcontroller.
  • Plant: DC motor with quadrature encoder providing velocity feedback.
  • Sampling Frequencies: Range of discrete rates from 10 Hz to 100 Hz.
  • Input: Step change from 0 → 4 rad/s with high-rate data logging.

Key Results

  • RMS tracking error decreases monotonically as sampling frequency increases.
  • Low sampling rates (~10–20 Hz) exhibit oscillations and sluggish response.
  • Performance gains diminish beyond ~50 Hz, indicating practical bandwidth limits.
  • Higher sampling frequencies reduce both overshoot and variability in settling time.
