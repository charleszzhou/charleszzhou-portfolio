MEMS Resonator Design (Comb-Drive Mass Sensor)

Design & COMSOL-Backed Modeling for Picogram Mass Detection

Executive Summary

Designed and analyzed a silicon MEMS comb-drive resonator for detecting picogram-scale mass changes via resonant frequency shifts. The work establishes feasibility limits and leverages COMSOL simulation for accurate effective stiffness and mass extraction.

Problem & Purpose

Detecting tiny mass changes (e.g., bacterial mass on the order of picograms) using MEMS resonators requires precise control of resonant behavior under electrical, mechanical, and fabrication constraints (e.g., ≤100 V actuation, ~1 µm minimum features). Simulation beyond lumped approximations is necessary to capture distributed stiffness and mode effects.

Approach

  • Analytical Constraints: Derived detectability limits by linking effective mass meff and stiffness k to measurable frequency shifts.
  • COMSOL Simulation: Used distributed structural mechanics models to extract fundamental mode shapes, effective stiffness, and effective mass, enabling reliable sensitivity estimates.
  • Tradeoff Insights: Identified when simple lumped approximations break down due to significant spring mass and mode-shape influences.

Key Outcomes

  • Feasibility constraints were established linking geometry and material choices to detectable mass resolution.
  • COMSOL-validated design point achieved a detectability margin ≥ 1 for target picogram masses.
  • Demonstrated that common simplified assumptions (e.g., meff ≈ 0.24mproof mass) do not hold under strict size and stiffness constraints for this geometry.

Tools & Skills

COMSOL · MEMS Modeling · Dynamics & Vibrations · Sensitivity Analysis · Design Tradeoffs
