MEMS Resonator Design (Comb-Drive Mass Sensor)

Design & COMSOL-Backed Modeling for Picogram Mass Detection

Objective

Design a silicon MEMS resonator capable of detecting picogram-scale mass changes via frequency shift, under strict electrical, mechanical, and fabrication constraints (e.g., ≤100 V, ~1 µm minimum features).

Context

  • Target organism: P. gingivalis (typical mass ~0.5–1.0 pg)
  • System concept: disposable saliva cartridge + reusable reader (this project focuses on the MEMS resonator)

Outcome

  • Established feasibility constraints linking detectability to meff, and actuation/stability to k
  • Identified why slide-level “lumped” approximations break for this geometry (spring inertia and mode shape are non-negligible)
  • Validated a COMSOL-backed design point with extracted parameters and detectability margin ≥ 1

Highlights

  • Why COMSOL is required: distributed stiffness, mode-shape-dependent inertia, and anchor compliance materially affect k and meff
  • Applicability check: “meff ≈ 0.24 mproofmass” only holds when proof mass ≫ 10× spring mass (not feasible under this design’s k/size constraints)
  • COMSOL extraction: use fundamental mode + energy/participation to compute meff and keff consistently for sensitivity calculations

Tools & Skills
