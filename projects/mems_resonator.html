MEMS Resonator Design (Comb-Drive Mass Sensor)

Comb-drive resonator for bacterial mass detection · analytical feasibility + sensitivity modeling · COMSOL-backed k and meff extraction

Objective

Design a silicon MEMS resonator capable of detecting picogram-scale mass changes via frequency shift, under strict electrical, mechanical, and fabrication constraints (e.g., ≤100 V, ~1 µm minimum features).

Context

  • Target organism: P. gingivalis (typical mass ~0.5–1.0 pg)
  • System concept: disposable saliva cartridge + reusable reader (this project focuses on the MEMS resonator)

Outcome

  • Established feasibility constraints (detectability upper-bounds meff; actuation/stability lower-bounds k)
  • Showed slide-level effective-mass approximation fails for this design regime (spring inertia not negligible)
  • Validated a COMSOL-backed design point with extracted parameters and a detectability margin ≥ 1

Highlights

  • Why COMSOL is required: distributed stiffness, mode-shape-dependent inertia, and anchor compliance materially affect k and meff
  • Applicability check: the “meff = 0.24 mproofmass” shortcut only holds when the proof mass ≫ 10× the spring mass (not feasible here)
  • Extracted (COMSOL): keff ≈ 3.92 N/m, meff ≈ 2.23 ng, f0 ≈ 211 kHz

Tools & Skills

COMSOL · MEMS Modeling · Dynamics & Vibrations · Sensitivity Analysis · Design Tradeoffs
