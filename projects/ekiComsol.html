Electrokinetic Injection Modeling in Microfluidics

COMSOL Simulation of EOF and Ion Transport

Executive Summary

Built a 2D COMSOL Multiphysics simulation capturing the electrokinetic injection (EKI) pinch–dispense sequence in a cross-channel microfluidic chip, enabling extraction of key experimental metrics such as plug center position, full width at half maximum (FWHM), and velocity.

Problem & Purpose

Electrokinetic injection in microfluidics couples electric fields, electroosmotic flow (EOF), and species transport. Accurately reproducing this process in simulation helps infer unknown parameters (e.g., zeta potential, effective diffusivity, pressure offsets) by matching model outputs to experimental plug evolution.

Modeling Approach

  • Physics Coupling: Electric currents, creeping flow (Stokes), and Nernst–Planck transport solved together to represent electrophoresis, EOF, convection, and diffusion.
  • Geometry: Depth-averaged 2D representation of the Micronit X8050 cross-channel chip.
  • Boundary Conditions: Electroosmotic slip applied at channel walls to model EOF.
  • Mesh Strategy: Convergence-focused refinement near the junction and initial downstream segment to stabilize plug tracking.

Key Outputs

  • Quantitative Plug Metrics: Extracted downstream plug center, FWHM evolution, and velocity time series.
  • Mechanism Separation: Demonstrated how EOF-driven convection and field-induced electrophoresis set plug velocity, while diffusion and field stretching determine broadening.

Tools & Skills

COMSOL Multiphysics · Microfluidics · Electroosmotic Flow · Electrophoresis · Nernst–Planck Transport · Mesh Convergence · Data Extraction
