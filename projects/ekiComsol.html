Electrokinetic Injection Modeling in Microfluidics

COMSOL Simulation of EOF and Ion Transport

Executive Summary

Built a 2D COMSOL Multiphysics model of electrokinetic injection (EKI) pinch–dispense in a cross-channel microfluidic chip. The simulation couples electric fields, flow, and species transport to reproduce plug formation and evolution, enabling direct extraction of plug center position, full width at half maximum (FWHM), and velocity for comparison to experiments.

Problem & Purpose

EKI couples applied voltages, electroosmotic flow (EOF), electrophoresis, and diffusion, making outcomes sensitive to parameters that are difficult to measure directly. A physics-based simulation helps interpret experiments by matching observed plug motion and broadening to infer effective properties (e.g., zeta potential, diffusivity, and pressure offsets).

Modeling Approach

  • Physics coupling: Electric currents + creeping flow (Stokes) + Nernst–Planck transport to capture EOF, electrophoresis, convection, and diffusion.
  • Geometry: Depth-averaged 2D representation of a cross-channel chip (Micronit X8050-style junction).
  • Boundary conditions: Electroosmotic slip at channel walls to model EOF-driven plug transport.
  • Mesh strategy: Targeted refinement near the junction and early downstream region to stabilize plug tracking and metric extraction.

Key Outputs

  • Quantitative plug metrics: Time series of downstream plug center position, FWHM evolution, and velocity.
  • Mechanism insight: Separated how EOF/electrophoresis set plug advection while diffusion and field-driven stretching govern broadening.

Tools & Skills

COMSOL Multiphysics · Microfluidics · Electroosmotic Flow · Electrophoresis · Nernst–Planck Transport · Mesh Convergence · Data Extraction
