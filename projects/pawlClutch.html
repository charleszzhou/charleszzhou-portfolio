Pawl Clutch for an Untethered Jumping Robot

Reliable Energy Release via Shape-Memory Alloy Actuation

Executive Summary

Designed and prototyped a compact pawl-based clutch with a Nitinol trigger to securely hold spring preload and release it cleanly on demand for an untethered jumping robot. The mechanism was iterated through rapid prototypes to improve engagement stability, release consistency, and repeatability.

Problem & Purpose

Jumping robots require a mechanism that can withstand high spring preload without slipping, then disengage reliably with minimal energy loss. The design challenge was balancing strong, robust engagement with a release action that is fast, repeatable, and tolerant to small misalignments and wear.

Design Highlights

  • Pawl engagement geometry: Tuned tooth/contact geometry for secure engagement and clean disengagement under load.
  • Nitinol trigger: Compact shape-memory actuator that retracts the latch to initiate release.
  • Friction & contact control: Guided motion paths and surface choices to reduce stiction and improve repeatability.
  • Rapid iteration: Multiple prototypes validated via benchtop cycling tests to converge on consistent performance.

Outcome & Validation

  • Consistent release under high spring preload across repeated trials.
  • Fast, compact actuation enabled by the Nitinol trigger.
  • Improved repeatability through geometry and friction tuning for reliable jumping sequences.
