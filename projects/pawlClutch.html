Pawl Clutch for an Untethered Jumping Robot

Reliable Energy Release via Shape-Memory Alloy Actuation

Executive Summary

Designed and prototyped a compact pawl-based clutch mechanism with a Nitinol trigger to reliably store and rapidly release spring energy for an untethered jumping robot.

Jumper Robot Clutch

Compact Pawl Clutch with Nitinol Trigger for Reliable Energy Release

Problem & Purpose

Jumping robots require a mechanism that can hold significant spring preload and then release it cleanly and consistently on demand. The design challenge was to balance secure engagement under load with repeatable disengagement and minimal energy loss.

Design Highlights

  • Pawl Clutch Geometry: Tuned for secure engagement and clean disengagement under repeated cycling.
  • Nitinol Trigger Actuation: Fast, compact actuation using shape-memory alloy wire to release the pawl.
  • Friction & Contact Optimization: Low-friction surface finishes and guided paths to improve consistency and reduce wear.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Iterated through multiple prototypes with benchtop testing to refine performance.

Outcome & Validation

  • Consistent release behavior under high spring loads across multiple trials.
  • Fast actuation enabled by the Nitinol trigger.
  • Improved repeatability through geometry and friction tuning, supporting reliable jumping sequences.
