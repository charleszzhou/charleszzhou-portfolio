How Sampling Frequency Shapes DC Motor Control Performance

Experimental study of discrete-time control effects on RMS tracking error and transient response

Objective

Quantify how controller sampling frequency influences closed-loop DC motor speed regulation, focusing on tracking accuracy and transient behavior in a discrete-time PI controller.

Experimental Setup

  • Discrete-time PI controller implemented on a microcontroller
  • DC motor with quadrature encoder velocity feedback
  • Sampling frequencies ranging from 10 Hz to 100 Hz
  • Step input from 0 → 4 rad/s with high-rate data logging

Key Results

  • RMS tracking error decreases monotonically with sampling frequency
  • Low sampling rates (10–20 Hz) exhibit oscillations and sluggish response
  • Performance gains diminish beyond ~50 Hz, indicating bandwidth limits
  • Higher sampling frequencies reduce overshoot and settling time variability
