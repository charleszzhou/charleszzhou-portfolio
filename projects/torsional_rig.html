Torsional Stiffness Measurement Apparatus

Low-Cost Rig for Quantifying Torsional Compliance in Soft Actuators

Executive Summary

Built a low-cost apparatus to measure torsional stiffness of soft robotic beams with high resolution (<0.1 Nm/deg) and repeatability, replacing the need for a $12 K torque sensor.

Problem & Purpose

Soft robotic structures often lack easily measurable torsional properties due to nonlinear compliance and expensive instrumentation. This rig provides a modular, low-cost method to quantify torque vs. angular deflection reliably across different soft actuators and pressurization levels.

System Overview

The apparatus uses a pulley-based torque translation mechanism coupled with a bearing-mounted protractor to measure angular displacement under applied torque. Swappable couplers allow testing of different actuator geometries and pressure states, with a consistent calibration workflow ensuring reproducible results.

Key Outcomes

  • High resolution & repeatability: Torque resolution ~0.1 Nm/deg with <±5 % variance across trials.
  • Modular design: Easily reconfigurable couplers accommodate multiple actuator sizes and loading conditions.
  • Validated trends: Demonstrated consistent stiffness–pressure relationships in inflatable soft beams, enabling quantitative comparison across designs.

Highlights

  • Modular, 3D-printed construction
  • Repeatable calibration methodology
  • Data analysis pipeline for torque–angle regression
